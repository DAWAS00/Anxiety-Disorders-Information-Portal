Types of Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders come in several forms, each with unique characteristics and challenges.

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Generalized Anxiety Disorder involves persistent and excessive worry about various aspects of life. People with GAD find it difficult to control their worry.

Key Characteristics:

  • Persistent worrying about many areas of life
  • Difficulty controlling worry
  • Worrying out of proportion to the actual risk
  • Physical symptoms like restlessness, fatigue, and muscle tension

Panic Disorder

Panic disorder is characterized by recurring unexpected panic attacks and persistent concern about having more attacks.

Key Characteristics:

  • Sudden episodes of intense fear
  • Physical symptoms like heart palpitations, shortness of breath, dizziness
  • Fear of losing control or "going crazy"
  • Avoidance of places where panic attacks have occurred

Social Anxiety Disorder

Social anxiety disorder involves intense fear of social situations and being judged or humiliated by others.

Key Characteristics:

  • Intense fear of social situations
  • Worry about being embarrassed or humiliated
  • Avoidance of social situations
  • Physical symptoms like blushing, sweating, trembling in social settings

Specific Phobias

Specific phobias are intense fears of particular objects or situations that pose little or no actual danger.

Common Types:

  • Heights (acrophobia)
  • Flying (aviophobia)
  • Spiders (arachnophobia)
  • Enclosed spaces (claustrophobia)
  • Blood or needles (hemophobia)
